Donald Copps dies at 76

Donald W. Copps, 76, former chair-
man of the board and chief executive
of the Copps Corp., died Tuesday
morning, Aug. 14, 1990, in Stevens
Point.

Copps had had a series of strokes
over the past two years.

After receiving a bachelor of
science degree from Carroll College,
Waukesha, he joined the Copps
Corp., representing the third genera-
tion in the family business.

Copps initiated the business’ entry
into fresh fruits and vegetables.
Upon becoming executive vice presi-
dent, he helped establish the first of
many corporately owned supermar-
kets, the first discount food stores
and the predecessor of the present-
day Copps Food Centers.

In 1970, he was named president
and chief executive officer; from
1977 until his retirement in 1980, he
was chairman of the board and CEO.

Copps was a director at large of
the Food Marketing Institute and a
member of the board of directors of
IGA.

In Stevens Point, he was a past
president of the Chamber of Com-
merce, which named him a Citizen of
the Year; founder of Stevens Point
Little League baseball, the Youth
Center and the Serra Club; and
leader of the drive to build the mu-
nicipal swimming pool, now named
in his honor, and to establish Pacelli
High School.

Copps was a member of the boards
of directors of Citizens National
Bank (now Bank One), Viterbo Col-
lege and Goodwill Industries of
North Central Wisconsin.

Surviving are his wife Mary Jane
and five children.

Services will be 10 a.m. Friday at
St. Stanislaus Church, Stevens Point.
