Robert Scott Jr., mortgage banker

BRIELLE — Robert E. Scott
Jr., 47, a borough resident who had
been promoted two months ago to
manage the finances of one of the
state’s largest developers, died Fri-
day at Elizabeth General Medical
Center, Elizabeth, after a sudden,
undisclosed illness.

f Mr. Scott was Vice President for
Finance at K. Hovnanian Enterpris-
es, Middletown Township, the
state’s largest home builder, and
president of its mortgage banking
subsidiary, R. E. Scott Mortgage Co.,
Elizabeth.

Among the survivors are his
oldest son, Robert E. III, who will be
graduated from Monmouth College
today, and Steven T., who will be
graduated from Christian Brothers
Academy, Middletown Township,
later this month.

Also surviving are his wife, the
former Barbara Hauser, owner of a
Brielle gift shop; a third son, Charles
W.; two brothers, Richard E., Red
Bank, and Randolph E., Eatontown;
and his mother, Ruth E. (Gabler)
Scott.

R.E. Scott was founded by Mr.
Scott’s father, Robert E. Scott Sr., in
1948, and was sold to K. Hovnanian
for an undisclosed price in 1983.
The younger Scott was president of
the company’s branches in Eaton-
town and Cranford for 22 years.

As president of R.E. Scott, he
supervised a staff of 65 and managed
a $250 million mortgage portfolio on
New Jersey and Florida properties.
He was also past president of two
affiliated companies, RESCO Devel-
opment Corp. and the R.E. Scott
School of Real Estate, both in Eliza-
beth, for many years.

“He was considered to be one of
the more energetic people in the in-
dustry,” said Frank L. Bowersox, ex-
ecutive vice president and chief op-
erating officer at R.E. Scott. “He had
a lot of plans in front of him.

“He was well respected and well
known in the business and well liked
by his staff,” Bowersox said. “He
was the kind of guy who worked
seven days a week, 12 hours a day.
We’re certainly going to miss him.”

Mr. Scott was past president of
the Eastern Union County Board of
Realtors, and the Educational Foun-
dation of the New Jersey Mortgage
Bankers Assoc., chairman of the
Taxation Committee of the
N.J.A.R., and the Legislative Com-_
mittee of the Mortgage Bankers
Assoc. of New Jersey, and of the
Mortgage Bankers Political Action
Committee of New Jersey.

In the mid-1970s, Mr. Scott was
a leader in efforts to reform property
taxes in New Jersey, often speaking
on the implications of property tax
relief on the home building industry.

Mr. Scott was co-chairman of
the legislative committee of the
N.J.A.R, the director of the New
Jersey Taxpayers Assoc., the Union
County Multiple Listings Service,
the Builders Assoc. of Metropolitan
New Jersey, and the Home-to-Home
Real Estate Network.

He was a member and youngest
president of the New Jersey Mort-
gage Bankers Assoc., Union, a mem-
ber of the Mortgage Bankers Assoc.
of America, the New Jersey Assoc. of
Realtors Boards, the New Jersey
Assoc. of Realtors, the N.J. Assoc. of
Home Builders, and the National
Assoc. of Home Builders.

Mr. Scott was a 1962 graduate
of Dartmouth College, earning a
B.A. in business; a 1964 graduate of
the American Institute of Real Es-
tate Appraisers and a 1966 graduate
of Realtors National Marketing in- —
stitute. He earned a New Jersey ~
Mortgage Banker license in 1982.

WALL TOWNSHIP — Robert E.
Scott Jr., 47, died Friday (May 20,
1988) at Elizabeth General Medical
Center.

He was born in Rahway and lived
in Scotch Plains, Lincroft and Manas-
quan before moving to the Brielle
section five years ago.

He was president for 22 years of
R.E. Scott Mortgage Co., formerly of
Elizabeth and now of Eatontown and
Cranford. He also was vice president
of finance for two months for K. Hov-
nanian Enterprises in Red Bank. Be-
fore that he was president for many
years of RESCO Development Corp.
and the R.E. Scott School of Real
Estate, both in Elizabeth.

He was a past president of the
Eastern Union County Board of Real-
tors in 1973 and the Educational
Foundation of the N.J. Mortgage
Bankers Association in 1978. He was
chairman of the Taxation Committee
of the N.J. Association of Realtors,
the legislative committee of the
Mortgage Bankers Association of
N.J. and the Mortgage Bankers Polit-
ical Action Committee of N.J., and
co-chairman of the legislative com-

mittee of the N.J. Association of
Realtors. He was director of the N.J.
Taxpayers Association, the Union
County Multiple Listing Service dur-
ing the 1970s, Builders Association of
Metropolitan N.J. in the 1980s and the
Home-to-Home Real Estate Net-
work,

He was the youngest president of
the N.J. Mortgage Bankers Associa-
tion and a member of the Mortgage
Bankers Association of America, N.J.
Association of Realtors Boards, the
National Association of Realtors, the
N.J. Association of Home Builders
and the National Association of Home
Builders.

He was a trustee for the Everett
Civic Association in Lincroft and a
member and trustee of the Manas-
quan River Marlin and Tuna Club.

Surviving are his wife, Barbara
Hauser Scott, three sons, Robert E.
III, Charles W. and Steven T., all at
home; two brothers, Richard E. of
Red Bank and Randolph E. of Eaton-
town, and his mother, Ruth E. Gabler
Scott.

Arrangements are by Leonard
Home for Funerals in Elizabeth.
